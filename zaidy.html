The story


Bubby's parents Myrtyl & Marie, and their children (other than Micheline) arrived in NY on March 25, 1939 from Le Havre France on the ship Normandia.
Marie’s mother Emilie came along with them.
Marie’s father Isaac Seligman was also supposed to come – a ticket had been purchased for him – but he died in 1939 before the family left to NY. (cousin albert Bader – same person signed nutr papers)











Bubby’s grandmother’s maiden name was also Dryfuss – she was an orphan. Bubby remembers going on walks with her grandmother in NY and helping her take baths. Her grandmother is buried in Long Island. That was the cemetery of the French shul they davened at in at the time.
Bubby’s father and aunt Gaby went back to France in 1947. Gaby wanted to go to medical school and could not get into school in the US. Bubby’s father died in Paris in 1950 and is buried there.

Bubby’s mother is buried in Breuer's section of King Solomon Memorial Park since they were affiliated with the Bruer Kehilla at the time.
Micheline was taken to China by her aunt Renee Battegay around 1938. Renee and her husband Mathieu were wealthy and traveled between China, Europe and the US many times.



After WWII, Bubby’s mother got Micheline out of China with the help of the Red Cross. She came to the US in March of 1947.

Renee (also through the help of Bubby's mother) came to the US in July of 1947.

A few years later, Renne went to Canada, with Micheline following her there. They lived and are burried in Montreal.
